Snapshots of Experience

Deutsch LA & Taco Bell

Objective: Plan and facilitate a research project designed to understand the attitudes and perceptions of bicultural Hispanic fast food consumers towards the Taco Bell brand. The discussion guide concentrated on the Live Mas campaign and the introduction of a breakfast menu. Research included testing creative concepts (60 sec spots and social media) with respondents and documenting reactions.

Tactics: Focus Group Moderation (English and Spanish), In-Home Ethnography Interviews, Cultural Branding, and Consulting

Results: Based on research and strategic consulting provided, Taco Bell decided to move more of their creative work to Deutsch LA. This allowed the planning department to build a “total market” approach within the agency to service more of their client roster, which includes 7Up, Dr. Pepper, and Volkswagen.

Project Coverage

PepsiCo, Huemanitas and Partner Agencies

Objective: Produce consumer research and insights into U.S. multicultural market in order to provide intelligent creative platforms for shopper marketing efforts, and develop a cultural branding model for senior PepsiCo leadership. This included developing creative marketing solutions across the organization’s portfolio of brands.

Tactics: Consumer Insights, Creative Ideation, Engagement Mapping, Sponsorship Partnership, Event Marketing, Content Development, Retail & Shopper Marketing, and Market Research

Results: PepsiCo improved it’s brand equity in the strategically important U.S. multicultural market. Today, a cultural branding strategy permeates throughout the organization and is applied in the form of cross-cultural marketing tactics. This applies to work produced by all partner agencies. It started with the total market approach, led by Hispanic consumers, at the shopper and retail marketing level.

Deutsch LA and Volkswagen

Objective: Uncover attitudes and perceptions of U.S. Hispanic consumers, especially Millennials, towards the Volkswagen brand during the new car buying process. Conversation focused on what is important while shopping for a new vehicle, including information on online research behavior, in person dealer visits, test driving, while also exploring brand heritage, and the role of language.

Tactics: Market Research, Focus Group Moderation (English and Spanish), and Consulting

Results: Agency leadership used cultural intelligence generated to present the VP of Marketing at Volkswagen of America with a strategy for engaging Hispanic car buyers. This included insights into how consumers absorb and interpret brand messaging, they’re cross-cultural Latin connection to brand’s heritage, digital media needs, and the role sponsorship assets, such as the motorsports, play in connecting with Hispanic Millennials.
